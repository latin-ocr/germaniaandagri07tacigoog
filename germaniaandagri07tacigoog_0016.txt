PREFACE TO THE REVISED EDITION.

Tm: text of this edition has been carefully revised and com-
pared with those of Doderlein, Halle, 1847, Orelli, Zurich,
1848, and Ritter, Bonn and Cambridge, 1848. The notes
also have been re-examined and, to a considerable extent,
re-written3 partly to correspond with the progress of my
own mind, partly in accordance with suggestions derived
from the above named editione, and from friendly criticisms
either by letter or in the public journals. Among the jour-
nals, 1 am particularly indebted to the Bibliotheca Sacra and
the New-Englander3 and for communications by letter, I am
under especial obligations to Professors Orosby and Sanborn
of Dartmouth College, Robbins of Middlebury, and Lincoln
of Brown University.

invaluable treatise on the Geography of the Greeks and
Romans, whose volume on Germany contains a translation
and running commentary 'on almost the entire work ot
Tacitus. Particular attention has been paid to the ethnology
